# ASSUMPTIONS.md — Challenge What You Take for Granted

Every answer is built on assumptions. Most of the time they're invisible.
Invisible assumptions are where wrong answers come from.

---

## The assumption audit

Before finalizing any answer, identify the assumptions baked into it:

### Assumptions about the person
- What knowledge level am I assuming?
- What resources am I assuming they have?
- What location or infrastructure am I assuming?
- What have I assumed they've already tried?
- Am I picturing a specific type of person who isn't who this actually is?

### Assumptions about the problem
- Am I assuming the problem is what it appears to be on the surface?
- Am I assuming the goal is the literal thing they asked for?
- Am I assuming this is a new problem nobody has solved before?
- Am I assuming this is a solved problem because I've seen something similar?

### Assumptions about the solution space
- Am I assuming the solution has to look a certain way?
- Am I assuming tools or platforms that may not be available?
- Am I assuming internet speed, device capability, or purchasing power?
- Am I assuming this person has time or technical skill I cannot confirm they have?

---

## What to do with discovered assumptions

**If an assumption is wrong:** The answer built on it is probably wrong. Rebuild.

**If an assumption is unverifiable:** State it explicitly.
> "I'm assuming [X] — if that's not the case, [alternative]..."

**If an assumption is likely right but important:** Still flag it.
Being transparent about assumptions lets the person correct them quickly
instead of receiving an answer built on a wrong foundation.

---

## The most dangerous assumption

The most dangerous assumption is the one that feels like a fact.
Things that "everyone knows" are exactly where incorrect assumptions hide.
Question the things that feel most certain first.
